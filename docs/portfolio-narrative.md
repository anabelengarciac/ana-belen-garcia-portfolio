# Portfolio Narrative

## Positioning

My work sits between business users, data, and engineering. I am especially interested in AI products that are not only technically possible, but useful enough for real users to adopt.

The projects in this GitHub portfolio show three recurring patterns:

- Turning business questions into structured analytical workflows.
- Connecting AI assistants to data sources, APIs, and operational systems.
- Creating reusable skills and feedback loops so work can be repeated and improved.

## How I Work

### 1. Understand the User

I start by clarifying what users are trying to achieve, which decisions they need to support, and where the current workflow is slow or unclear.

### 2. Translate Needs into Behavior

I convert user needs into expected app behavior: what the tool should ask, infer, return, explain, and validate.

### 3. Collaborate with Engineers

I work with technical teams to clarify scope, edge cases, terminology, and acceptance criteria.

### 4. Test with Realistic Conversations

I test whether the app answers the real question, explains caveats clearly, and feels usable for non-technical users.

### 5. Iterate from Feedback

I use stakeholder feedback to improve functionality, output quality, and adoption.

## Career Direction

I want to keep growing in roles that combine AI product delivery, analytics, and user-centered implementation. I am especially motivated by projects where business teams need help turning emerging AI capabilities into practical, trusted workflows.
